\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper investigated whether the CS2 skin market is integrated with
traditional and crypto financial markets. Using a daily value-weighted price
index from 1,607 liquid skins over the period August 2013 to June 2024, we
tested for integration against the S\&P~500, Bitcoin, and Gold via EDA,
GARCH(1,1), and a VAR(4) with Granger causality tests.

\textbf{The answer is clear: the CS2 skin market is not integrated with any of
the three comparison assets.} Contemporaneous correlations are indistinguishable
from zero and no Granger causal relationship is detected at the 5\% significance
level in either direction. H1 and H2 are rejected; H3 is partially supported ---
CS2 shares Bitcoin's volatility persistence ($\hat{\alpha}+\hat{\beta}=0.9990$)
but differs markedly in shock reactivity. The near-zero correlation with all
mainstream assets implies meaningful diversification potential for investors
already holding equity or crypto positions \cite{dobrynskaya2025videogame}, but
extreme annualised volatility (114.56\%) and the absence of price-discovery
linkages to broader markets make CS2 skins a high-risk, idiosyncratic asset
unsuitable as a standalone hedge.

The main limitations are the restriction to Steam Marketplace data (excluding
third-party platforms such as Skinport or CS.Money), the value-weighting bias
towards liquid skins, and the potential structural break at the CS:GO-to-CS2
engine transition in September 2023. Future work should test for this breakpoint
explicitly, estimate a pairwise CS2--Bitcoin VAR as a robustness check, and
incorporate data from third-party platforms and equal-weighted benchmarks to
obtain a more complete picture of CS2 skin price dynamics.
