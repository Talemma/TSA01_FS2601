\section{Literature Review}
\label{sec:literature}

The CS2 skin market has emerged as a candidate financial asset class. Operating
through the Steam Marketplace --- an open secondary market with real-currency
pricing --- it reached an estimated \$4.1~billion in value by early 2025
\cite{guedefernandez2025ai}. Unlike competing titles that restrict trading to
developer-set prices, CS2's open market allows supply and demand to determine
prices, making skins suitable for standard financial analysis
\cite{zhou2024cs2valorant}. \citeauthor{dobrynskaya2025videogame} document
average annual returns of 41.2\% with a Sharpe ratio of 0.34 across nearly
5,000 CS skins from 2013 to 2024 --- exceeding both the US equity market (0.25)
and gold (0.12) \cite{dobrynskaya2025videogame}. These findings motivate the
central question of whether the CS2 market operates independently of, or
co-moves with, mainstream financial assets.

Bitcoin serves as the primary speculative comparator. \citeauthor{baur2018bitcoin}
find that Bitcoin is held predominantly for speculative purposes, with returns
uncorrelated with traditional asset classes under both normal and stressed market
conditions \cite{baur2018bitcoin}. \citeauthor{bouri2017hedge} confirm that
Bitcoin offers minimal hedging properties, with its portfolio value lying in
diversification rather than risk reduction \cite{bouri2017hedge}. The shared
demographic between Bitcoin investors and CS2 skin traders --- young, digitally
native, and risk-tolerant --- motivates the hypothesis that crypto market
sentiment may Granger-cause skin prices. The most plausible transmission
mechanism is a wealth channel: rising Bitcoin prices increase the disposable
income of the crypto-adjacent gamer demographic, stimulating demand for CS2
skins and potentially driving prices upward with a short lag. \citeauthor{akin2024fluctuations},
using a VAR framework equivalent to the one applied here, find weak but
statistically detectable interactions between Bitcoin, the S\&P 500, and gold,
establishing the empirical context against which CS2's behaviour is measured
\cite{akin2024fluctuations}.

The methodological toolkit draws on three established approaches. Vector
Autoregression, introduced by \citeauthor{sims1980macroeconomics}, captures
joint dynamics across multiple time series without imposing a-priori causal
structure \cite{sims1980macroeconomics}. Granger causality
\cite{granger1969causal} operationalises directional predictability in a
linear framework: $X$ Granger-causes $Y$ if past values of $X$ improve
forecasts of $Y$ beyond what $Y$'s own history provides. GARCH(1,1) \cite{bollerslev1986garch} models time-varying
conditional variance and its clustering properties, enabling cross-asset
comparison of shock persistence.
