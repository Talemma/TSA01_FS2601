\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Counter-Strike 2 (CS2) is one of the most played competitive shooters in the
world. Since 2013, players have been able to trade purely cosmetic weapon
modifications --- ``skins'' --- through the Steam Marketplace at freely
negotiated, real-money prices. Unlike the closed monetisation systems of
competing titles, Steam operates an open secondary market, making CS2 skins
an observable and quantifiable asset class. By early 2025, the ecosystem had
grown to over 22,000 tradeable items with an estimated market value of
\$4.1~billion \cite{guedefernandez2025ai}. Prior work has found attractive
risk-adjusted returns and low correlation with traditional assets
\cite{dobrynskaya2025videogame}, but no study has formally tested market
integration using a VAR and Granger causality framework on a
comprehensively constructed price index.

This paper fills that gap. We construct a daily value-weighted CS2 price
index from raw Steam Marketplace transaction data and ask:

\textit{``Is the CS2 skin market integrated with traditional and crypto
financial markets, and what is the direction and temporal dynamics of any
such relationship?''}

We test this against the S\&P~500, Bitcoin, and Gold --- benchmarks for
traditional equity, speculative crypto, and safe-haven performance --- using
EDA, GARCH(1,1) volatility modelling, and a VAR model with Granger causality
tests.
