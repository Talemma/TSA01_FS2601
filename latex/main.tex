\begin{abstract}
\noindent
This paper examines whether the CS2 skin market --- a multi-billion-dollar
virtual goods market operating through the Steam Marketplace --- is integrated
with traditional and crypto financial markets. We construct a daily
value-weighted price index from 1,607 liquid CS2 skins over the period August
2013 to June 2024 and test for market integration against the S\&P 500,
Bitcoin, and Gold using exploratory data analysis, GARCH(1,1) volatility
modelling, and a VAR(4) model with Granger causality tests. We find no evidence
of market integration: contemporaneous correlations between CS2 returns and all
three comparison assets are indistinguishable from zero, and no Granger causal
relationship is detected in either direction at the 5\% significance level.
CS2 exhibits extreme annualised volatility (114.56\%) and GARCH persistence
identical to Bitcoin (0.9990), but its price dynamics are driven by
game-specific factors rather than macroeconomic forces. These results position
CS2 skins as a genuinely segmented alternative asset class with potential
diversification benefits for financial investors.
\end{abstract}
